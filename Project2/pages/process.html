Process

I started my design process by planning out how the pages would attempt to look like. This consisted of drawing out simple wireframes on paper (See Below). There were 5 sketches in total, each representing their own unique page. The wireframes were rough guidelines to show where content can be oriented. In the first image, I had mapped out my IA on the header and footer sections. It was relatively easy as there only needed to be 5 distinct pages to map out. When designing the pages, I took inspiration from the sites of popular brands such as Apple and Nike. It helped to frame images like my home page. With the final iteration of my pages, they were not one-to-one with my inital vision since some ideas I could not code properly.

In regards to accessibility and usability, I used techniques taught in lecture as well as the "Wave Accessibility Feature" on Firefox. As taught in class, I put alt-text, labels, titles, and captions for each media asset that needed one. Furthermore, I used the Wave Accessibility Extension to capture any problems that go against the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (Excluding the alert posed by the YouTube video).
